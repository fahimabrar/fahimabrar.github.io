Emotion Identification in Financial Market

The interplay between emotion and sentiments are the basis of many trading decision. Thus, this FinTech-AI project aims to utilise novel AI and semantic techniques to develop new predictive emotion-based algorithms that utilise financial news.

Hypothesis
If there are negative emation (Anger, Fear, Sad etc.) conveyed by the news articles the stock price should go down in the next days, If there are positive emotion (Trust, Joy etc.) the stock price should go up.

contributions
- Significantly improved correlations between the stock price vs the emotion. In some cases it is up to 43 percent.
- Develped a automatic domain specific keywords and their emotion values.
- The developed tools can be adapted to any other domain.
- Calculated the tolarance of different companies toward negative news, and devleloped metrics of thershold.

Captioning Images for Disabled People- Using Deep Learning

According to the World Health Organization, more than one billion people are suffering from some sort of disability and the number is increasing day by day (World Health Organization, 2011). Regarding the accessibility of web information, most of the web remains inaccessible or contains some sort of difficulties to access for disabled people (Adam and Kreps, 2006). This project is to develop a captioning system for visually impaired people.

A total of six models have been developed based on different Convolutional Neural Networks and Recurrent Neural Network based architectures. Different types of word embeddings along with their strengths and weakness have been discussed. The models are evaluated with appropriate matrices, and the best model is selected for real world implementation. The highest BLEU score achieved from the model is 0.54. Further improvements to the model are also proposed, based on the gained knowledge while conducting the research.
