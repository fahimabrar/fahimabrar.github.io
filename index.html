Research & Publications

📄

Artificial Neural Network and Machine Learning Based Methods for Population Estimation of Rohingya Refugees: Comparing Data-Driven and Satellite Image-Driven Approaches

Journal: Vietnam Journal of Computer Science
Publisher: World Scientific
DOI: 10.1142/S2196888819500246

Manual census collection is slow and costly in refugee settings, so this study explored faster alternatives: estimating the Rohingya refugee population with remote sensing and machine learning. It benchmarked 11 models, including artificial neural networks, across a data-driven and a satellite image-driven approach. Gradient Boosting performed best on the data-driven side and proved suitable for both, while the ANN performed best on satellite imagery.

🎓

Deep Learning Based Image Captioning for Visually Impaired Web Users

MSc Dissertation · Data Science & Analytics
Institution: Brunel University London (2021)

Developed an encoder-decoder image captioning system to describe website images for visually impaired users, connecting computer vision with natural language processing. Six models were built across different CNN and RNN architectures, word embeddings were compared, and the best model (BLEU 0.54) was selected for real-world implementation at CityMaas.

Common Questions

Where does our client data go?
Wherever possible I build inside your own accounts and infrastructure, so your documents never leave your control. Confidentiality constraints are scoped in writing before any build starts. I've built systems handling sensitive legal and healthcare data, so I know what's at stake.
What does an AI audit involve?
A fixed-scope review of where your team's hours actually go. I map the workflows, quantify the potential savings in hours and £, and hand you a prioritised roadmap. You can act on it with me or without me. There's no obligation beyond the audit itself.
Do we need technical staff?
No. I build, integrate, train your team and hand over documentation. If you have IT people, I'll work with them; if you don't, that's fine; most of my clients don't.
How long does a project take?
A single automation: days to a couple of weeks. A knowledge assistant or CRM build: typically a few weeks, delivered in fixed scopes, so you'll always know the timeline before we start.
What kinds of businesses do you work with?
Small businesses. That's a deliberate choice, not a limitation. I've delivered in professional services, healthcare, HR and education, and small teams are where one well-built system makes the biggest difference. If your problem is repetitive knowledge work, it probably fits, whatever your industry.
